# Software Design

**1. Core Definition**
Software Design is the strategic translation of domain requirements into maintainable, correct code structures. It is the bridge between design theory and practical implementation, shifting development from haphazard coding to professional engineering.

**2. Complexity Management**
The primary objective is to control complexity through two distinct lenses:
1. **Essential Complexity:** Inherent to the domain; cannot be removed, only addressed.
1. **Accidental Complexity:** Artificial constraints arising from tools or enterprise layers; must be minimized.

**3. Engineering Constraints**
Effective design requires a continuous balance between three competing factors:
1. **Requirements:** Meeting both functional and non-functional specifications.
1. **Project Goals:** Achieving immediate delivery milestones.
1. **Sustainability:** Reducing long-term maintenance costs.

**4. Methodological Approach**
To ensure professional engineering, the process prioritizes:
1. **Architectural Focus:** Treating architecture as the foundational layer for all decision-making.
1. **Informed Choice:** Making deliberate, conscious decisions at every level of development.
1. **Paradigm Integration:** Utilizing complementary paradigms (e.g., Object-Oriented Design and Functional Declarative Design) to decouple components and reduce technical risk.

**5. Strategic Objectives**
The ultimate goal is the cultivation of **design intuition**, characterized by:
1. **Tool Agnosticism:** Prioritizing conceptual techniques over language-specific features.
1. **Paradigm Fluidity:** The ability to shift between paradigms based on the problem's needs.
1. **Knowledge Transfer:** Utilizing artifacts (diagrams, DSLs) to enable team alignment.

#### **6. Summary Equation**
**Software Design** = (Paradigm-Guided Translation) + (Systematic Engineering) -> Complexity Management -> Maintainable Systems
