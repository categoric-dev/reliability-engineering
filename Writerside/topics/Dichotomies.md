# Systemic Dichotomies

## System Stability & Reliability
1. **Dependability vs. Resiliency**
    *   **Dependability:** Prevention of failure; ensuring safe operation under normal conditions.
    *   **Resiliency:** Recovery from failure; the capacity to absorb disturbances and maintain continuity during disruption.
1. **Resiliency vs. Robustness**
    *   **Resiliency:** Focuses on **recovery** (returning to state after an event).
    *   **Robustness:** Focuses on **persistence** (sustaining performance under stress/variability). 
   *Note: Robustness is the aggregate of dependability, resiliency, fault-tolerance, and adaptive control.*
1. **Resilience vs. Reliability**
    *   **Resilience:** The ability to withstand and recover from faults.
    *   **Reliability:** The probability of behaving as expected over time (encompasses resilience, availability, and maintainability).

## Validation & Consistency
*   **Repeatability vs. Reproducibility**
    *   **Repeatability:** Consistency of results obtained under *identical* conditions (same operator, equipment, and environment).
    *   **Reproducibility:** Consistency of results obtained under *varying* conditions (different operators, equipment, or environments), proving generalizability.

## Risk Management
*   **Acceptance vs. Mitigation**
    *   **Acceptance:** A formal decision to retain residual risk because it falls within established organizational or regulatory thresholds.
    *   **Mitigation:** The active implementation of controls (engineering, procedural, or administrative) to reduce residual risk below an acceptable threshold.

## Lifecycle Management
*   **Manageability vs. Maintainability**
    *   **Manageability:** Ease of modifying system behavior at **runtime**.
    *   **Maintainability:** Ease of modifying system functionality via **code/design changes**.
